Ph.D. Student · Computer Vision · Multimodal Learning

Di Wen

I develop robust, deployment-oriented human perception systems for fine-grained action understanding in industrial, assistive, and open-world settings.

I am a Ph.D. student in the CV:HCI Lab at Karlsruhe Institute of Technology, advised by Prof. Rainer Stiefelhagen. Since April 2024, my research has focused on video-based human perception for fine-grained action understanding, human-object interaction, and robust multimodal systems that operate under noisy labels, limited supervision, and open-world deployment conditions. My work is aligned with SFB 1574-B04's focus on human perception for circular factory environments, and I am open to research collaborations, student projects, and thesis supervision.

Focus

Research Interests

Fine-Grained Human Action Understanding

Temporal segmentation, referring atomic actions, procedural activities, and human-object interaction in complex video.

Robust & Open-World Perception

Reliable learning under noisy labels, uncertainty, limited supervision, domain shift, and unknown situations.

Deployment-Oriented Industrial/Assistive AI

Data, benchmarks, and multimodal systems for circular factories, wearable assistants, robotics, and unusual environments.
