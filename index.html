Ph.D. Student · Computer Vision · Multimodal Learning

Di Wen

I build robust vision-language systems for fine-grained human action understanding in noisy, open-world, and deployment-oriented settings.

I am a Ph.D. student at the CV:HCI Lab, Karlsruhe Institute of Technology. My research focuses on action recognition and segmentation, human-object interaction, robustness, uncertainty, and multimodal perception for real-world assistive systems.

Focus

Research Interests

Robust Action Understanding

Fine-grained recognition, temporal segmentation, noisy labels, uncertainty, and evaluation under realistic distribution shifts.

Human-Object Interaction

Modeling atomic actions, object-centric reasoning, and reliable perception in complex human-centered scenes.

Deployment Datasets

Building benchmarks and data resources for robotics, industrial assembly, assistive systems, and unusual environments.
